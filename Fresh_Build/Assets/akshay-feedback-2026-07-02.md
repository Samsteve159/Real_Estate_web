The pages that change frequently are Listings, Projects and Insights. Everything else is written as evergreen content that stays relevant for some years.

For Manifest, I'd avoid writing about current market conditions or "we currently have..." because those date the website quickly. Instead, write about your expertise, process and the value you provide.

Here's how I'd structure each page.

## Home

The homepage should answer four questions immediately:
- Who are you?
- What do you do?
- Why choose you?
- What should I do next?

## One additional suggestion

Instead of calling the navigation item "Advisory", consider "Property Advisory" or "Services". "Advisory" alone can feel vague, whereas "Property Advisory" immediately signals value and expertise.

Overall, I'd keep the top navigation concise: Home · Listings · Property Advisory · Development Projects · About · Contact

This gives visitors a clear path while allowing all pages except Listings to remain evergreen with little or no need for future rewrites.
